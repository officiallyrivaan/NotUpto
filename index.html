The Problem

What "Up To" really means

That tiny phrase does enormous legal work. "Up to 2× faster" means the device was faster — once, in one test, in one lab. The rest of the time? Nobody's saying.

🎯
"Up To" is a ceiling, not an average
It represents a best-case, cherry-picked scenario — rarely reproducible in everyday use. You'll never see the worst case advertised.
⚖️
It's technically legal
Regulatory bodies allow "up to" claims as long as the number is achievable under some condition. Brands exploit this loophole aggressively.
🔍
Real performance tells the actual story
Sustained brightness, real-world battery, and actual benchmark gains — that's what you'll carry home. NotUpto finds those numbers.
